# TidyTuesday 2026-06-23: Papal Encyclicals

![From Leo XIII to Leo XIV: Shared Words, Different Weight](26_shared_vocabulary_dumbbell.png)

## About

This chart compares vocabulary in two full-text encyclicals from the TidyTuesday papal encyclicals dataset:

- **Rerum Novarum** by **Pope Leo XIII**, published in **1891**
- **Magnifica Humanitas** by **Pope Leo XIV**, published in **2026**

The plot shows the top 20 words that appear in both letters, ranked by their frequency in Leo XIV's 2026 text. Common stopwords and a few generic terms were removed, and close variants were merged, for example `rights` into `right`, `men` into `man`, and `humanity` into `human`.

## Reading The Chart

Each row is one shared word. The teal dot is the 1891 frequency, the orange dot is the 2026 frequency, and the connecting line shows how much the word's weight changes between the two texts.

The strongest shift is toward explicitly human-centered language. Words such as **human**, **dignity**, **responsibility**, **peace**, and **power** appear far more often in the 2026 letter. Older social teaching vocabulary remains visible through words like **work**, **right**, **justice**, and **church**, but the relative emphasis moves from labor-era concerns toward broader questions of human dignity and responsibility.
